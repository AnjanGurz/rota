Grammar must be learned in sequence. Each level builds on the previous. Follow this exact order — skipping ahead creates gaps that cost you marks.

Level 1 — You need these to say anything (Month 1)

  • Être + avoir — present tense, all pronouns. Memorize completely.
  • -ER verb conjugation — applies to 90% of French verbs
  • Articles — le/la/les (definite), un/une/des (indefinite)
  • Negation — ne...pas around the verb
  • Basic questions — intonation rising at the end

Level 2 — You need these to talk about time (Month 2)

  • Passé composé — avoir + past participle (j'ai mangé)
  • Être verbs in passé composé — DR MRS VANDERTRAMP
  • Futur proche — aller + infinitive. Je vais partir demain.
  • Adjective agreement — masculine/feminine/plural
  • -IR and -RE verb patterns (finir, vendre)

Level 3 — You need these for natural French (Month 3)

  • Imparfait — background past. C'était beau. Il habitait là.
  • PC vs Imparfait — the #1 tested grammar point in TEF writing
  • Direct object pronouns — le, la, les (Je le mange)
  • Indirect object pronouns — lui, leur (Je lui parle)
  • Connectors — mais, donc, parce que, cependant, en revanche, ainsi

Level 4 — You need these for CLB 7 (Month 4)

  • Futur simple — all verbs. Je mangerai, tu iras, elle sera.
  • Conditionnel — polite + hypothetical. Je voudrais, ce serait bien.
  • Subjonctif — key triggers: il faut que, je veux que, bien que
  • Relative pronouns — qui, que, dont, où in complex sentences
  • Gérondif — en mangeant, en travaillant (while doing something)

How to study each grammar point — 5-day method

  • Day 1: understand the rule — WHY it works, not just how
  • Day 2: write 10 sentences using it about your own life
  • Day 3: speak those sentences out loud until automatic
  • Day 4: find it in your RFI listening — notice it in real French
  • Day 5: use it in your morning speaking block without thinking

Most people study French. Very few study the TEF exam format. Knowing the format can move your score by a full CLB level on its own. Study this from Month 3.
TEF Canada — exam structure
  • →Listening: 60 questions, ~40 minutes — multiple choice
  • →Reading: 50 questions, 60 minutes — multiple choice + gap fill
  • →Writing: 2 tasks, 60 minutes — message + opinion essay
  • →Speaking: 3 tasks, ~15 minutes — recorded, not live with examiner
  • →CLB 7 = roughly 60–70% in each section
Listening strategy
  • →Read all answer choices BEFORE the audio plays — always
  • →Mark your best guess immediately — never leave blank
  • →Audio plays once only. No repeats. Train for this right now.
  • →Answers often use synonyms — the exact word from audio may not appear
  • →Focus on: WHO, WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, WHY in every clip
Reading strategy
  • →Read the question first, then scan the text for the answer
  • →72 seconds per question maximum — practice this pace from Month 3
  • →If stuck after 60 seconds: best guess, move on, come back
  • →Unknown words → use context clues from surrounding sentences
  • →The answer is always in the text — never from outside knowledge
Writing — essay structure to memorize
  • 1Introduction: state the topic + your position (2-3 sentences)
  • 2Body 1: first argument + example (3-4 sentences)
  • 3Body 2: second argument + example (3-4 sentences)
  • 4Conclusion: restate position + open question (2 sentences)
  • →Connectors to memorize cold: D'abord · Ensuite · De plus · Cependant · En conclusion
  • →Target 200 words minimum. Count every practice essay.
Speaking — monologue structure
  • →You get a topic + 1 minute to prepare. Use every second of it.
  • →Structure: position → reason 1 → reason 2 → nuance → conclusion
  • →Say "c'est-à-dire" and "en effet" — sounds natural and fills thinking time
  • →Speak slowly and clearly — examiners reward clarity over speed
  • →Mistakes are fine — stopping and restarting kills your score. Keep going always.
Your 3 drift triggers: phone scrolling in the morning, switching to dev work, random tab-hopping. Every rule below kills one of these specifically.

Against phone scrolling

  • →Phone charges in kitchen every night — not bedroom, not desk. Kitchen.
  • →Alarm clock wakes you. Phone has no reason to be touched until 6:30 AM.
  • →When the urge hits at 4 AM — say out loud: "pas maintenant." Open laptop.
  • →First 10 minutes are the danger zone. Survive them and you won't want to stop.

Against switching to dev work

  • →Only ONE browser tab open during study — the one you are working in.
  • →Dev work, GitHub, everything else — closed before you sit down at 4 AM.
  • →Write on paper and tape to your desk: "Dev work after 5:30 PM only."
  • →Dev idea hits during study → write one line on paper, return immediately. Ideas don't disappear. Focus does.

Against tab-hopping and "quick checks"

  • →Night before: open exactly the file you need. Sleep the laptop. 4 AM = it's already there.
  • →No quick checks exist. A quick check is 20 minutes minimum. You know this.
  • →Physical timer — set for 45 minutes. Not allowed to switch until it rings.
  • →End every session by writing tomorrow's first sentence on paper. Brain has nothing to negotiate with.

The reset — for bad mornings like today

  • →Scrolled and lost the morning → don't punish yourself. It's already gone.
  • →Do the evening block. One focused 40 minutes still moves you forward.
  • →Tonight: prep properly. Tomorrow: 4 AM alarm. Start clean. No guilt.
