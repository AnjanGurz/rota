Grammar in sequence. Each level builds on the previous. Follow this exact order.

Level 1 — say anything (Month 1)

  • Être + avoir — present tense, all pronouns. Memorize completely.
  • -ER verb conjugation — applies to 90% of French verbs
  • Articles — le/la/les, un/une/des
  • Negation — ne...pas around the verb

Level 2 — talk about time (Month 2)

  • Passé composé — avoir + past participle (j'ai mangé)
  • Être verbs — DR MRS VANDERTRAMP
  • Futur proche — aller + infinitive
  • Adjective agreement — masculine/feminine/plural

Level 3 — natural French (Month 3)

  • Imparfait — background descriptions, habits in past
  • PC vs Imparfait — #1 tested grammar in TEF writing
  • Object pronouns — le, la, les, lui, leur
  • Connectors — mais, donc, parce que, cependant, en revanche

Level 4 — CLB 7 (Month 4)

  • Futur simple — all verbs. Je mangerai, tu iras, elle sera.
  • Conditionnel — polite + hypothetical. Je voudrais, ce serait.
  • Subjonctif — il faut que, je veux que, bien que
  • Relative pronouns — qui, que, dont, où

These 3 techniques are built into every daily todo. Read this once so you know exactly what you are doing and why.

Shadowing — for speaking and pronunciation

Listen to native French and speak at the exact same time — not after. Copy rhythm, speed, sound. Forces your brain to produce French without translating from English. 5 minutes daily beats 1 hour of free practice.

How: RFI episode → one sentence → press play → speak simultaneously → repeat 3 times → next sentence.

Sentence mining — for vocabulary

Pick a real sentence from your RFI episode or class slide with one unknown word. Add the full sentence to your flashcard — not just the word. Brain retains words in context 4x better.

How: Find sentence with ONE unknown word → add to Flashcards tab → front = sentence, back = meaning + pronunciation + variation.

Teach-it-back — for grammar

After reviewing a grammar rule, close your notes and explain it out loud in simple terms — as if teaching a beginner. If you cannot explain it simply, you do not know it yet.

How: Review notes → close them → say out loud: "Okay so this rule works like this..." → 2 minutes → stuck = the gap to re-read.

Active recall — for everything

Cover your notes. Write from memory. Uncover and check. Never just re-read. The act of trying to remember — even failing — is what builds long-term memory.

Gender mastery — le / la / les

French gender is not logical — it must be memorized with the article. Every noun you learn, learn with its article. The Drills tab has daily gender mini-drills. 5 nouns per day = 150 per month.

Knowing the TEF format can move your score by a full CLB level. Study this from Month 3.
TEF Canada — structure
  • →Listening: 60 questions, ~40 min — multiple choice
  • →Reading: 50 questions, 60 min — multiple choice + gap fill
  • →Writing: 2 tasks, 60 min — message + opinion essay
  • →Speaking: 3 tasks, ~15 min — recorded, not live
  • →CLB 7 = roughly 60–70% in each section
Listening strategy
  • →Read answer choices BEFORE audio plays — always
  • →Audio plays once only. Train for this with RFI now.
  • →Answers use synonyms — exact word may not appear
  • →Focus: WHO, WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, WHY in every clip
Reading strategy
  • →Question first, then scan text for answer
  • →72 seconds per question maximum
  • →Stuck after 60s: best guess, move on, come back
Writing — essay structure
  • 1Intro: topic + your position (2-3 sentences)
  • 2Body 1: argument + example (3-4 sentences)
  • 3Body 2: argument + example (3-4 sentences)
  • 4Conclusion: restate + open question
  • →Connectors: D'abord · Ensuite · De plus · Cependant · En conclusion
Speaking — monologue
  • →1 minute prep — use every second
  • →Structure: position → reason 1 → reason 2 → nuance → conclusion
  • →Fill silence: C'est-à-dire... / En fait... / En effet...
  • →Keep going even with mistakes — stopping kills your score
TCF Canada is shorter and adaptive. Both TEF and TCF accepted for immigration. Decide which one you sit by Month 3.
TEF vs TCF
  • →Both accepted for Express Entry / PR
  • →TEF: longer, fixed difficulty — better if strong in grammar
  • →TCF: shorter, adaptive — better if strong in listening + speaking
  • →Decide by Month 3, then mock test that format only
TCF Canada — structure
  • →Listening: 29 questions, ~35 min — adaptive difficulty
  • →Reading: 29 questions, ~60 min — adaptive difficulty
  • →Writing: 2 tasks, 60 min — message + structured essay
  • →Speaking: 3 tasks, ~12 min — recorded
  • →Adaptive = harder questions = you're doing well. Don't panic.
Writing — TCF structure
  • 1Task 1: message or letter (60-80 words)
  • 2Task 2: structured opinion (180-200 words minimum)
  • →Connectors: Premièrement · De plus · En outre · Cependant · Pour conclure
Key differences from TEF
  • →TCF adaptive vs TEF fixed difficulty
  • →TCF fewer questions but each counts more
  • →TCF speaking uses images — TEF is spoken topics only
  • →TCF results in ~15 days. Check test centre in Thunder Bay.
